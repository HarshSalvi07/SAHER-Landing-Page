About Us

Born from the ashes of conflict.

Founded in Jogeshwari — scarred by the communal violence of 1992–1993 — SAHER began as an interfaith cricket club and grew into a movement for lasting peace. Today we bridge divides across Mumbai's most polarized communities through education, sport, and civic action.

OUR MISSION

Peace is not merely the absence of war.

India's hundred languages, twenty religions, and multitude of cuisines come together daily — yet these same differences are weaponised to trap communities in cycles of suspicion and conflict. SAHER enables young people to think critically, foster respect for diversity, and become active, boundary-less citizens — working at the impressionable stage, before ideas calcify into prejudice. Our vision: a world where differences are recognised, accepted and appreciated.

Our Programmes

🕊️ PARWAAZ

A structured journey of self-exploration through citizenship education, conflict management, reproductive health, gender issues, and sustainable development.

🛠️ NEENV

Life skills and employability training covering communication, financial literacy, legal awareness, and technical vocational skills. Includes Tameer — a Labour Resource Bank.

🏘️ MOHALLA HAMARA

A community initiative where participants learn about civic issues, the Indian Constitution, and engage public institutions to address health, sanitation, and access to basic services..

⚽ SPORTS 4 PEACE

Using football as an anchor, this initiative builds friendship beyond stereotypes, promotes gender awareness, unity, and shared decision-making.

Support Our Cause

1. Reach young people early

We work with adolescents and youth — still at an impressionable age — before social identities solidify into rigid barriers.

2. Create structured exposure

Sustained interventions, Curriculum-based modules, community mapping, sports, and residentials all create sustained, safe exposure to diverse realities..

3. Build active citizens

Every programme culminates in citizenship action — participants take what they've learnt and initiate change in their own communities.
